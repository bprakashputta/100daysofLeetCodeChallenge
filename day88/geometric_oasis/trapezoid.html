Trapezoid


Trapezoid has two parallel of sides of different lengths a and b

Perimeter of Trapezoid : length1 + length2 + 2*(side) = a+b+2*side

Area of Trapezoid : 1/2*(length1+length2)*height = 1.2*(a+b)*h

Height of Trapezoid : Perpendicular drawn between two opposite sides of Trapezoid



In Euclidean geometry, a convex quadrilateral with at least one pair of parallel sides is referred to as a trapezium in English outside North America, but as a trapezoid

The parallel sides are called the bases of the trapezoid and the other two sides are called the legs or the lateral sides (if they are not parallel; otherwise there are two pairs of bases).

A scalene trapezoid is a trapezoid with no sides of equal measure.
