Triangle


Triangle has three sides with lengths a, b, and c

Perimeter of Triangle : (side1 + side2 + side3) = (a+b+c)

Area of Triangle : (1/2)*(base)*(height)

height of Triangle : 2*(Area/base) = (2*A)/b



Triangle comes from the Latin word triangulus, "three-cornered" or "having three angles," from the roots tri-, "three," and angulus, "angle or corner."

Heron's formula, formula credited to Heron of Alexandria (c. 62 ce) for finding the area of a triangle in terms of the lengths of its sides.
