Rectangle


Rectangle has two pairs of sides with equal lengths l and b

Perimeter of Rectangle : 2*(length + breadth) = 2*(l+b)

Area of Rectangle : (length)*(breadth) = l*b

Length of diagonal : √(length*length + breadth*breadth) = √(l²+b²)



The word rectangle comes from the Latin rectangulus, which is a combination of rectus (as an adjective, right, proper) and angulus (angle).

it was invented by the Egyptians and Mesopotamians although the Greeks made geometry books that were from the Egyptians.

Carl Friedrich Richard Foerste invented the formula of perimeter of rectangle

In the 7th century CE, Brahmagupta developed a formula, now known as Brahmagupta's formula, for the area of a cyclic quadrilateral (a quadrilateral inscribed in a circle) in terms of its sides.
